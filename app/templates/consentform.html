Brief introduction of the experiment procedure

{% if quiz_type == 'pilot_1' %}

If you agree to participate, you will be randomly assigned with two other participants to form a group of three, and will answer 10 questions about your own attitudes towards some public issues. After answering each question, all the participants' answers will be disclosed to each other. After the experiment, you will be asked to place yourself and the other two participants on an ideology spectrum. Therefore, please be aware of the other two participants' answers. In the whole process, your identity will be kept anonymous.

{% elif quiz_type == 'pilot_2' or quiz_type == "pilot_2_2" %}

In this survey, you will be asked 26 questions on your ideology, hard facts, predictions of the future, some potentially controversial public issues and some design problems.

{% elif quiz_type == "condition_1" %}

If you agree to participate, you will be randomly assigned to a group of three participants. You and the other two participants will answer a sequence of 6 questions on either hard facts, or future predictions, or potentially controversial issues. For each question, one participant will be randomly chosen and answer the question first. Then her/his answer, together with the question, will be disclosed to the other two participants. In the whole process, your identity will be kept anonymous.

{% else %}

This experiment consists of two phases. In each phase you will answer a set of questions about hard facts, future predictions, personal preferences or attitudes towards public issues.

  • Phase 1 contains 18 questions. You will answer them on your own.

  • Phase 2 contains 10 questions. We will randomly draw two previous participants from the dataset of our earlier experiments. Their answers to each question will be shown to you after you submit your own answer.

  • Between Phase 2 and Phase 3, you will be asked about how you think of the two previous participants. So, please be aware of their answers in Phase 2.

  • In Phase 3, you will be asked 6 questions. For each question, either you will answer it on your own or we will show you a previous participant's answer for your reference.

You will see detailed instructions during the experiment. In the whole process, your identity will be kept anonymous.

Your participation is completely voluntary

Taking part in this study is completely voluntary. If you decide to participate, you are free to withdraw at any time and you are free to stop at any time for any reason. However, should you decide to withdraw, you will forfeit your reward.

Your identity is completely anonymous

We re-emphasize that your identity and responses will be completely anonymous. We do not collect any personally identifiable information. Your test responses will be stored on an encrypted database. Only researchers involved in this study will have access to this information. The records will not contain your name or any other personal information that could be used to identify you. In any report we make public, we will only discuss group averages and will not include any individual information.
